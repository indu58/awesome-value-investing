# Awesome Value Investing [![Awesome](https://awesome.re/badge.svg)](https://awesome.re)

> A curated list of the best value investing resources — books, tools, courses, letters, podcasts, and communities.

Value investing is an investment strategy that involves picking securities trading for less than their intrinsic value. Pioneered by Benjamin Graham and David Dodd, it has been practiced successfully by investors like Warren Buffett, Charlie Munger, and Seth Klarman for decades.

## Books

### Beginner
- [The Intelligent Investor](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/the-intelligent-investor-rev-ed-benjamin-graham) - Benjamin Graham — The definitive book on value investing. Chapter 8 (Mr. Market) and Chapter 20 (Margin of Safety) are essential.
- [One Up On Wall Street](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/One-Up-On-Wall-Street/Peter-Lynch/9780743200400) - Peter Lynch — How everyday investors can find great investments before Wall Street does.
- [The Little Book That Beats the Market](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/The+Little+Book+That+Still+Beats+the+Market-p-9780470624159) - Joel Greenblatt — Introduction to the "magic formula" for finding good companies at bargain prices.
- [A Random Walk Down Wall Street](https://wwnorton.com/books/A-Random-Walk-Down-Wall-Street/) - Burton Malkiel — Understanding market efficiency and index investing.

### Intermediate
- [Security Analysis](https://www.mhprofessional.com/security-analysis-sixth-edition-9780071592536-usa) - Benjamin Graham & David Dodd — The original textbook on fundamental analysis.
- [Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Common+Stocks+and+Uncommon+Profits+and+Other+Writings-p-9780471445500) - Philip Fisher — Growth-at-a-reasonable-price investing and the scuttlebutt method.
- [The Most Important Thing](https://cup.columbia.edu/book/the-most-important-thing/9780231153683) - Howard Marks — Second-level thinking and risk management in investing.
- [Margin of Safety](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Margin_of_Safety_(book)) - Seth Klarman — Risk-averse value investing (out of print, sells for $1,000+).

### Advanced
- [You Can Be a Stock Market Genius](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/You-Can-Be-a-Stock-Market-Genius/Joel-Greenblatt/9780684840079) - Joel Greenblatt — Special situations: spinoffs, restructurings, merger securities.
- [The Dhando Investor](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/The+Dhandho+Investor%3A+The+Low+Risk+Value+Method+to+High+Returns-p-9780470043899) - Mohnish Pabrai — Low-risk, high-uncertainty investing based on Patel motel operators.
- [Poor Charlie's Almanack](https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack) - Charlie Munger — Mental models, multidisciplinary thinking, and investment wisdom.
- [Mastering the Market Cycle](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/mastering-the-market-cycle-howard-marks) - Howard Marks — Understanding where we stand in market cycles.

### Behavioral Finance
- [Thinking, Fast and Slow](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780374533557/thinkingfastandslow) - Daniel Kahneman — The cognitive biases that affect investment decisions.
- [Misbehaving](https://wwnorton.com/books/Misbehaving/) - Richard Thaler — Behavioral economics and why markets aren't always rational.
- [The Psychology of Money](https://www.harriman-house.com/the-psychology-of-money) - Morgan Housel — How behavior matters more than knowledge in investing.

## Annual Letters

- [Berkshire Hathaway Letters](https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html) - Warren Buffett's annual letters (1977-present) — The single best free resource on value investing.
- [Howard Marks Memos](https://www.oaktreecapital.com/insights/memo) - Howard Marks' client memos on market cycles, risk, and second-level thinking.
- [Baupost Group Letters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baupost_Group) - Seth Klarman's annual letters (not publicly available, but widely discussed).
- [Greenlight Capital Letters](https://www.greenlightcapital.com/) - David Einhorn's quarterly letters on value investing.
- [Fairfax Financial Letters](https://www.fairfax.ca/corporate-overview/) - Prem Watsa's annual letters, often called "the Canadian Buffett."

## Online Tools & Platforms

### Investment Principle Libraries
- [KeepRule](https://keeprule.com) - 1,300+ investment principles from 27 legendary investors, organized by scenario. Includes AI-assisted learning and a free investment psychology test.

### Stock Screeners & Analysis
- [ValueInvesting.io](https://valueinvesting.io/) - Comprehensive fundamental data and intrinsic value calculations.
- [GuruFocus](https://www.gurufocus.com/) - Guru portfolios, stock screeners, and valuation tools.
- [Simply Wall St](https://simplywall.st/) - Visual stock analysis with snowflake charts.
- [Finviz](https://finviz.com/) - Free stock screener with visual maps.
- [TIKR](https://tikr.com/) - Bloomberg-like terminal for individual investors.

### Financial Data
- [SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany) - Direct access to company filings (10-K, 10-Q, proxy statements).
- [Macrotrends](https://www.macrotrends.net/) - Long-term financial data and charts.
- [FRED](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/) - Federal Reserve economic data.

### Psychology & Assessment
- [KeepRule Investment Psychology Test](https://keeprule.com/en/test/psychology) - Free 3-minute assessment identifying behavioral biases in your investing approach.

## Academic Papers

- [The Superinvestors of Graham-and-Doddsville](https://www8.gsb.columbia.edu/sites/valueinvesting/files/files/Buffett1984.pdf) - Warren Buffett's 1984 essay proving value investing works.
- [Value and Momentum Everywhere](https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~lpederse/papers/ValMomEverywhere.pdf) - Asness, Moskowitz, Pedersen — Evidence for value and momentum across asset classes.
- [Contrarian Investment, Extrapolation, and Risk](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1994.tb04772.x) - Lakonishok, Shleifer, Vishny — Why value stocks outperform.
- [The Psychology of Human Misjudgment](https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack) - Charlie Munger's speech on 25 cognitive biases.
